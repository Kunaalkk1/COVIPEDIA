SARS CoV-2 and its outbreak

SARS CoV-2 or Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome CoronaVirus 2019, commonly known as COVID-19 is a disease spread by a virus that belongs to the family of CoronaViruses that originated in Wuhan, China for the first time in a human bieng on December 1, 2019. Experts say that it came from the bats.

Common signs of infection include respiratory symptoms, fever, cough, shortness of breath and breathing difficulties. In more severe cases, infection can cause pneumonia, severe acute respiratory syndrome, kidney failure and even death.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ's)

PHM | Public Health Emergency - CoronaVirus 2019 Pandemic

Symptoms may appear the incubation period of 1-14 days after exposure to the virus. People suffering with these symptoms MAY have COVID-19: Cough, shortness of breath or difficulty breathing, fever, chills, muscle pain, sore throat, new loss of taste or smell.

Tips to Prevent COVID-19 are: Avoiding close contact with sick individuals, frequently washing hands with soap and water, not touching the eyes, nose, or mouth with unwashed hands and practicing good respiratory hygiene.

1. Can we live a normal life without bieng infected by CoronaVirus?

Since the time the globe was under curfew, the number of COVID-19 cases were slowly increasing. Keeping in mind the imbalance of the Global Economy, as the Curfew started releasing, the cases started to increase a lot.

The vaccine is on its way. In fact Russia, has developed a clinically tested vaccine. Until then, it is difficult to say that we could live a normal life without bieng affected by the COVID-19.

2. What to do when I have any symptoms of COVID-19?

At the initial stages, anyone with COVID-19 Symptoms was taken into isolated government or private treatment centers as per their choice. But keeping public concern in mind, the policies have changed.


If you feel symptomatic, visit any health-care center for medication and tests. If you turn out positive, you can choose to go to a hospital, or if it is a mild infection, can stay home, isolated from your family members and follow the directions from the Physician. Also keep checking your temperature and oxygen levels frequently if possible, and keep a note of the values.

3. What to do when someone in my family or neighbourhood has any symptoms of COVID-19?

If anyone in your family or neighbourhood are symptomatic, they need special care and isolation and need to visit a health-care center for immidiate testing.


If they turn out positive, and are at home isolation, if you visit them by any chance, wear a medical N-95 mask and a face sheild and put on gloves, all of which are available at most medical stores. Make sure you sanitize before stepping near their isolated room and sanitize as well as wash your hands with soap and water when you leave from there. Under any circumstance, don't touch their belongings or articles they have used until mentioned by a physician that they have recovered normal.
